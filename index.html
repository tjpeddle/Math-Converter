How it works
1
Upload any Word document created with the equation editor — exams, worksheets, problem sets.
2
Auto-detects every OMML math formula in the document, including fractions, exponents, polynomials, and factored forms.
3
Inserts spoken text directly below each formula in bold italic — no label, just natural classroom language Word reads aloud correctly.
4
Formula stays visible. The student sees the equation and hears the spoken version. Word's Read Aloud skips the OMML object and reads the spoken line naturally.
Spoken math conventions used
x²→x squared
x³→x cubed
x⁴→x to the fourth power
x⁻²→x to the negative 2
(3x+4)→the quantity 3x plus 4
a/b→a over b
x⁰→x to the zero power
g(x)→g of x
